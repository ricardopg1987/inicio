W-ENER Program

Online Meetings of the Worldwide Energy Network

W-ENER is an international network of virtual conferences that connects experts, researchers and professionals in the field of sustainable energy from around the world, promoting knowledge exchange and global collaboration.

About W-ENER

The W-ENER (Worldwide Energy Network) program was born in response to the need to maintain active international scientific collaboration during times when global mobility was restricted. Through periodic virtual meetings, W-ENER facilitates the exchange of ideas among researchers from different countries and continents.

This virtual format has democratized access to high-quality scientific content, eliminating geographical barriers and reducing costs, which facilitates participation from students and researchers at institutions with limited resources.

Program Objectives

  • Create a global network of sustainable energy researchers
  • Facilitate access to cutting-edge scientific knowledge
  • Promote international collaboration in research projects
  • Foster exchange of best practices and experiences
  • Strengthen links between academic institutions worldwide
  • Support continuous training of researchers and professionals

Meeting Format

W-ENER meetings are held virtually through videoconference platforms, on a monthly or bimonthly basis. Each session includes:

  • Keynote Lectures: Presentations by invited experts on current topics
  • Research Presentations: Results from ongoing projects
  • Q&A Sessions: Direct interaction between speakers and audience
  • Virtual Networking: Spaces for connection between participants
  • Recordings: Available for later viewing

Thematic Areas

W-ENER covers a broad spectrum of topics in sustainable energy:

  • Renewable energies: solar, wind, hydroelectric, geothermal, biomass
  • Power electronics and energy systems control
  • Energy storage and battery management
  • Microgrids and distributed energy systems
  • Green hydrogen and synthetic fuels
  • Energy efficiency and demand management
  • Electric and sustainable mobility
  • Energy policies and energy transition
  • Environmental impact and life cycle analysis

Participation

W-ENER meetings are open to researchers, academics, graduate students, industry professionals and anyone interested in sustainable energy. Participation is generally free, with prior registration.

Participants can propose topics for future sessions and apply as speakers to share their research with the global W-ENER community.

International Network

W-ENER has established collaborations with research institutions and universities from various countries, including:

  • Chilean and Latin American universities
  • European institutions specializing in renewable energy
  • Asian research centers in power electronics
  • North American laboratories in sustainable energy
  • International networks such as IEEE PES and IEC

Impact and Achievements

Since its creation, W-ENER has:

  • Conducted over 50 virtual sessions with participants from 5 continents
  • Facilitated the formation of international research collaborations
  • Reached over 2,000 unique participants
  • Generated globally accessible educational content
  • Promoted visibility of emerging researchers

Organization

The W-ENER program is coordinated by the Laboratory of Energy Conversion and Power Electronics (LCEEP) at Universidad de Talca, in collaboration with a network of partner institutions internationally.

How to Participate

To receive information about upcoming W-ENER meetings and register, send an email to w-ener@lceep.cl or subscribe to our mailing list.
